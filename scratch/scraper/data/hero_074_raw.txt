Background
[
]
A steadfast protector of
Jiang
, Da Qiao is also a very caring companion. She was once forced to marry someone out of filial piety, but on the night before her wedding, she met
Sun Ce
while she was standing on the shore, looking out to the sea in sorrow. If she is the port that he comes home to, then he is the light that brings her out of her darkness. Her controlling of the tides to protect this land is to fulfill her lover's dream and to exercise her will.
Lore
[
]
Ancient Anomalic: The Qiao clan of Wu adhered to complex and strict traditions in order to maintain the sorcery passed down through their bloodline. But their worst fear came to pass: the birth of the first twin girls in thousands of years seemed to fulfill the prophecy that the Qiao clan would die. Terrified of what would come to pass, the elders made a cold-blooded decision: the older sister would be groomed as the heir, and the younger would be erased from the family tree and sent to
Jixia
.
Under her family's strict upbringing, Da Qiao became an elegant maiden, perfect in everything except her refusal to approach the ocean. To her people's displeasure, she never showed the strength they expected from an heir. Could they have made the wrong decision? Only the patriarch, her grandfather, remained stubbornly adamant. When he died, his last words, "bride of the sea", left everyone confused.
At that time, the coast was in turmoil. When the rulers of Wu learned of Da Qiao's reputation, they desired her. Having reached the end of their rope, the Qiao clan decided to offer him this beautiful yet useless tribute. Having lost her only defender, her grandfather, and with her sister out of reach in Jixia, Da Qiao agreed to sacrifice herself for the family, but on one condition—that before her wedding, she be allowed to go to the seaside and pray to her grandfather. And so, her clansmen agreed to her request.
She carried a lamp onto the rocks, in a place where the waves spoke in a way only she could understand. She listened calmly to the waves, preparing herself for what fate had in store. All she wanted was to protect the tiny light she had. It was her only hope of changing her destiny.
Neither the shroud of night nor the thick clouds could hide the faint, stubborn light, keeping the clansmen reassured that she could not escape. It was late, but at dawn, after three days of exhausting rituals, the end would come. They would send off the bride of the sea in exchange for glory for their clan.
Suddenly, the tide rose, rousing the guards. No, it wasn't the tide! It was a fleet! The ship that ambushed Jiang was coming ashore! It should have been lost in the dark sea, but the light had guided it through the whirlpools and the tides. The gamble had paid off! The panicked people had no idea that Wu was welcoming a new ruler and that this surprise attack would end the war.
Sun Ce, the first to jump off the ship, strode towards the lamp bearer, his constant companion following closely behind.
The young man gave the maiden his hand... And the memory of her confident pose would forever remain with him.
...
...
...
Time flew, and as the saying goes, all good things must come to an end. When the Eastwind Altar lit up, the maiden came out from seclusion and watched the light pierce the sky. It was the first time the lamp had flickered since the departure of her loved ones.
The last heir to the Qiao sorcerer clan had a surprisingly unyielding and stubborn streak to her. Not once did she think of surrendering to her oppressive fate. Now it would seem she again had the opportunity to reverse the impossible.
"I watch over the sky, the sea... and your memories."
